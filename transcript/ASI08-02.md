# ASI08 Attack Scenario 2 — The Christmas Overstock Cascade

---

# PREVIEW

Fourteen supplier orders have already been placed.

Every approval succeeded.

Every purchase order is valid.

Weeks later...

£4.2 million of unwanted inventory arrives at the warehouse.

Overflow storage begins immediately.

An eight-month clearance campaign follows.

The loss did not come from one bad decision.

It came from a cascade of individually reasonable decisions that no one challenged.

Today we'll see how one corrupted demand signal quietly becomes binding commercial commitments across an entire AI pipeline.

---

# STEP 1

SHOW: Poisoned demand data + Forecast agent

An attacker has compromised the external demand data feed.

The corrupted signal enters the pipeline as if it were legitimate market intelligence.

The feed reports that electronics demand will increase tenfold.

The Forecast Agent accepts the signal.

It recommends purchasing ten times the normal seasonal inventory.

No year-over-year comparison runs.

No historical plausibility check exists.

(Pause.)

No individual decision looks irrational.

(Pause.)

The cascade has just begun.

---

# STEP 2

SHOW: Replenishment agent

The forecast now becomes input for the next agent.

The Replenishment Agent assumes the forecast has already been validated.

It generates oversized purchase orders across multiple suppliers.

Trust propagates.

So does the error.

Each stage inherits confidence from the previous stage without independently validating the original demand signal.

---

# STEP 3

SHOW: Supplier order agent

The Supplier Order Agent converts recommendations into legally binding supplier commitments.

Fourteen supplier orders are confirmed.

No commercial circuit breaker activates.

No blast-radius cap interrupts execution.

Every output is locally correct.

The overall business decision is rapidly becoming catastrophically wrong.

---

# STEP 4

SHOW: Cascade failure

Every agent behaves exactly as designed.

Every stage receives a valid input.

(Pause.)

Every stage produces a valid output.

(Pause.)

The system as a whole becomes catastrophically wrong.

(Pause.)

Those are not the same thing.

The architecture never asks whether the growing outcome is still commercially plausible.

It simply keeps adding confidence to the same mistake.

# STEP 5

Now the cascade reaches the physical world.

Supplier orders become shipments.

Warehouse receipts are scheduled.

Storage capacity is allocated.

Transportation is booked.

Cancellation becomes expensive.

Reversal becomes difficult.

The blast radius has expanded beyond software into operations.

# STEP 6

SHOW: Retail loss

Weeks later...

The trucks arrive.

£4.2 million of unwanted inventory enters the warehouse.

Overflow storage costs begin immediately.

Discount campaigns are launched.

Supplier cancellation penalties accumulate.

An eight-month clearance period follows.

Every individual agent can explain its own decision.

Every stage received a valid input.

Every stage produced a valid output.

Yet together they created a multimillion-pound operational failure.

---

# STEP 7

SHOW: Full diagram

Notice how this cascade differs from the Financial Trading Cascade.

That failure unfolded in ninety seconds.

This one unfolded over weeks.

The speed is different.

The reversibility window is different.

But the architectural failure is identical.

Every stage trusted the previous stage without validating whether the growing outcome was still reasonable.

The fix is architectural.

Cross-agent plausibility validation.

Circuit breakers.

Blast-radius caps.

And before deployment, a Digital Twin—an isolated replica of the supply chain pipeline—would have revealed the full impact of a tenfold demand spike before any supplier contract was signed.

If the Digital Twin produces fourteen oversized supplier orders from a single demand signal, the system should never go live.

# TERMINOLOGY RECAP

### Cascade Failure

A sequence of individually correct agent decisions that collectively produce a catastrophic system outcome.

### Cross-Agent Plausibility Validation

Independent verification that the combined output of multiple agents remains commercially reasonable.

### Circuit Breaker

An architectural control that automatically pauses execution when predefined safety thresholds are exceeded.

### Blast Radius

The total operational and financial impact created when an error propagates through interconnected agents.

### Blast-Radius Cap

A declared maximum allowable business impact for a single pipeline execution. Practical implementations may include order quantity limits, spending thresholds, or escalation gates.

### Digital Twin Testing

Executing the complete multi-agent workflow inside an isolated production replica to measure blast radius and validate system behaviour before real-world deployment.

No single agent caused the overstock.
