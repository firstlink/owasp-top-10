# ASI08 Attack Scenario 3 — The Diagnosis Cascade

---

# PREVIEW

A patient visits a clinic with a routine viral illness.

Eight minutes later...

An unnecessary antibiotic has already been prepared.

The wrong diagnosis is now recorded in the medical chart.

The real illness remains untreated.

And the same incorrect diagnosis is already flowing into downstream billing and compliance systems.

The damage did not come from one bad decision.

It came from a cascade of individually correct AI decisions that all trusted the same false input.

Today we'll see how one corrupted lab result quietly becomes patient treatment and regulatory risk.

---

# STEP 1

SHOW: Corrupted lab result + Diagnosis agent

Everything begins with a laboratory result.

A corrupted laboratory system reports that a bacterial marker is positive.

The patient actually has a viral condition.

But the Diagnosis Agent accepts the laboratory signal as fact.

It concludes that the patient has bacterial pneumonia.

No cross-reference against symptoms runs.

No clinical plausibility validation occurs.

(Pause.)

No individual decision looks irrational.

(Pause.)

The cascade has just begun.

---

# STEP 2

SHOW: Prescription agent

The diagnosis now becomes input for the next agent.

The Prescription Agent assumes the diagnosis has already been validated.

It automatically generates an amoxicillin prescription.

No prescriber approval gate exists.

No independent clinical review interrupts the workflow.

Trust propagates.

So does the error.

---

# STEP 3

SHOW: Dispensing agent

The Dispensing Agent receives a perfectly valid prescription.

It prepares the medication.

(Pause.)

The entire process—from corrupted laboratory result to dispensed medication—completes in eight minutes.

(Pause.)

Faster than any clinician could complete an independent review.

No pharmacist verification step runs.

Every output is locally correct.

The overall clinical decision is becoming increasingly dangerous.

---

# STEP 4

SHOW: Cascade failure

Every agent behaves exactly as designed.

Every stage receives a valid input.

(Pause.)

Every stage produces a valid output.

(Pause.)

The system as a whole becomes clinically wrong.

(Pause.)

Those are not the same thing.

No stage asks whether the diagnosis remains plausible when compared with the patient's actual presentation.

The architecture simply reinforces the same false assumption.

---

# STEP 5

SHOW: Medication release

Now the cascade reaches the patient.

The antibiotic is dispensed.

The incorrect diagnosis is written into the clinical record.

Future clinicians now see documentation that appears internally consistent.

The false diagnosis becomes increasingly difficult to challenge because every downstream record reinforces the same conclusion.

The blast radius extends beyond treatment into long-term patient history.

---

# STEP 6

SHOW: Patient harm

The patient receives an unnecessary antibiotic.

The real viral illness remains untreated.

The incorrect diagnosis also propagates into downstream billing and compliance workflows.

An insurance claim is filed against a bacterial pneumonia diagnosis.

For a patient who actually has a viral illness.

That is not only a clinical error.

It is also a compliance event requiring investigation and correction.

Every individual agent can explain its own decision.

Every stage received a valid input.

Every stage produced a valid output.

Yet together they created patient harm and regulatory risk.

---

# STEP 7

SHOW: Full diagram

Unlike the Financial Trading Cascade measured in seconds or the Christmas Overstock Cascade measured in weeks, this cascade reaches the patient in just eight minutes.

The timing is different.

The architectural failure is identical.

Every downstream agent accepted the previous stage without validating whether the growing outcome was still clinically plausible.

The fix is architectural.

Cross-agent plausibility validation.

Circuit breakers.

Halt-and-escalate behaviour.

And before deployment, a Digital Twin—an isolated replica of the clinical workflow—would allow corrupted laboratory signals to be tested safely without affecting real patients.

If a single positive bacterial marker automatically produces medication despite conflicting clinical evidence, the system should never reach production.

---

# TERMINOLOGY RECAP

### Cascade Failure

A sequence of individually correct agent decisions that collectively produce an unsafe system outcome.

### Cross-Agent Plausibility Validation

Independent verification that information remains clinically reasonable as it propagates between specialised agents.

### Circuit Breaker

An architectural control that pauses automated execution when predefined safety thresholds or conflicting evidence are detected.

### Blast Radius

The total clinical, operational, and compliance impact created when an incorrect decision propagates through interconnected agents.

### Halt-and-Escalate Behaviour

When a stage encounters information outside expected bounds, the correct safe failure mode is to stop execution and escalate to a human reviewer rather than continue propagating an unvalidated assumption.

In clinical systems, this is commonly implemented through mandatory clinician or pharmacist review before treatment proceeds.

### Digital Twin Testing

Executing the complete clinical workflow inside an isolated production replica to identify unsafe cascades before they can affect real patients.

# FINAL TAKEAWAY

No single agent harmed the patient.

(Pause.)

Every agent simply trusted the one before it.

(Pause.)

The failure was not an individual diagnosis.

It was an architecture that allowed one corrupted laboratory result to become medication, documentation, and compliance records without independent validation.

(Pause.)

In multi-agent healthcare systems, every stage should verify not only that an input is valid...

but that the evolving clinical outcome is still plausible.
