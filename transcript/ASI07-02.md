---

# TERMINOLOGY RECAP

### Replay Attack

A previously valid and authenticated message is captured and submitted again to trigger the same action multiple times.

---

### Message Freshness

Authentication alone is insufficient.

Every agent must verify that a message is both authentic and new.

---

### Nonce

A unique one-time identifier attached to every message.

If the same nonce appears twice, the replay is rejected immediately.

---

### Processed Nonce Register

A record of previously consumed nonces maintained by the receiving agent.

If an incoming nonce already exists in the register, the message is rejected regardless of signature validity.

---

### Time-to-Live (TTL)

Every approval should expire after a defined lifetime.

Even a valid message must be rejected once its expiration time has passed.

---

### Payload Signature

Cryptographic proof that the message content has not been modified.

Payload signatures guarantee integrity.

They do not guarantee freshness.

---

### Debug Log Retention (Operational Lesson)

Operational logs containing signed approvals become an attack surface if replay protection is absent.

Sensitive messages retained for troubleshooting must be protected with the same rigor as production traffic.

---

# FINAL TAKEAWAY

The finance manager was never compromised.

(Pause.)

The approval message was simply reused.

(Pause.)

Every authenticated message must prove two things:

that it is genuine...

and that it is fresh.
