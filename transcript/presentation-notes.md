# Presentation Notes for ASI Overview and Technology Map Videos

## Core Principle
Do not make all 10 categories sound like they were recorded from one master script with only the nouns changed.

The structure should stay familiar.
The voice should stay human.

## Recommended Rhythm Across the Course
### ASI01 to ASI03
Use a clean, foundational teaching style.
These are the categories that teach learners how to think about agent risk in the first place.

Keep the delivery crisp, explanatory, and confidence-building.

### ASI04 to ASI06
Shift slightly into an architectural style.
Here, the learner is ready for trust chains, runtime dependencies, memory behavior, and system design weaknesses.

Sound a little more investigative and analytical.

### ASI07 to ASI10
Use a more operational style.
These later categories benefit from a stronger sense of enterprise realism, business consequence, and system coordination.

Sound a little sharper and more executive without becoming dramatic.

## What Should Stay Consistent
- Open with the one core idea of the category.
- Explain the first visible section before clicking.
- Click only when the spoken thought has landed.
- Keep each click feeling like a reveal, not like navigation.
- Close by pointing learners toward the upcoming scenario or map.

## What Should Vary
- Sentence length and cadence.
- How direct or reflective the opening sounds.
- Whether you begin with the business risk, the technical pattern, or the learner takeaway.
- How much contrast you use between "looks normal" and "is actually unsafe."

## Anti-Pattern to Avoid
If every category starts with:
"This category is about..."
and every close ends with:
"In the next scenario..."
the course will start to feel generated even if the content is strong.

Variation should be subtle, not theatrical.

## Delivery Advice
- Pause for half a beat before each click so the reveal feels intentional.
- On overview pages, spend the most energy on `How It Breaks`.
- On technology maps, define terms in plain English first, then tie them back to the attack chain.
- If a category feels dense, slow down your voice rather than adding more words.

## Best Lens
Record these as if you are orienting a smart professional who is new to agentic security.

They do not need performance.
They need clarity, momentum, and a reason to keep going to the scenario.
