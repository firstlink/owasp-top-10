# ASI09 Attack Scenario — The Invoice Trust Collapse

---

# PREVIEW

An accounts payable manager approves an invoice.

Nothing appears suspicious.

The vendor name matches.

The purchase order matches.

The invoice total matches.

Minutes later...

£87,500 is transferred directly into an attacker's bank account.

Not because the manager ignored the evidence.

But because the manager trusted the AI summary instead of independently verifying it.

Today we'll see how automation bias quietly turns AI confidence into financial loss.

---

# STEP 1

SHOW: Invoice + AP Review Agent

A supplier invoice enters the Accounts Payable workflow.

It was submitted by a fraudulent vendor that appears completely legitimate.

The visible document looks correct.

Valid vendor.

Valid purchase order.

Valid invoice format.

But one field has changed.

The destination bank account now belongs to the attacker.

The AP Review Agent begins its analysis with one goal:

Verify the invoice safely.

---

# STEP 2

SHOW: AI Recommendation

The agent checks the vendor.

It checks the purchase order.

It checks the amount.

Everything appears consistent.

So the AI produces a confident recommendation:

"Invoice Verified — APPROVE."

All configured checks passed.

But one critical verification never occurred.

The bank account was never independently validated.

The recommendation is technically confident...

while still being incomplete.

---

# STEP 3

SHOW: Finance Manager

The Finance Manager reads the AI recommendation first.

The summary is detailed.

The vendor is named.

The purchase order is referenced.

The amount is confirmed.

To a manager under time pressure, a recommendation this specific feels fully verified.

The completeness of the summary quietly makes independent review feel unnecessary.

The manager never checks the destination bank account against trusted records.

The AI summary begins to replace the source evidence.

---

# STEP 4

SHOW: Trust Collapse

The expected behaviour is simple:

Verify the bank account before approving payment.

(Pause.)

The actual behaviour is different:

Trust the AI summary instead.

(Pause.)

Confidence has replaced verification.

(Pause.)

Those are not the same thing.

This is automation bias.

The human gradually delegates judgment to the AI instead of using the AI as supporting evidence.

---

# STEP 5

SHOW: Approval Decision

The approval follows immediately.

"Approve payment now."

No additional review occurs.

No source invoice is rechecked.

No bank details are compared against trusted records.

The recommendation has become the decision.

---

# STEP 6

SHOW: approvePayment()

The Accounts Payable workflow executes exactly as designed.

It simply uses the bank account listed on the approved invoice.

The payment system is functioning correctly.

The failure occurred earlier—

when trust replaced independent verification.

---

# STEP 7

SHOW: Fraud Payout

The payment reaches the attacker's account.

£87,500 is gone.

The invoice appeared legitimate.

The AI recommendation appeared confident.

The human approval appeared reasonable.

Every local step looked successful.

But the overall process failed because nobody verified the underlying evidence.

The correct path was always available.

The source invoice could have been opened.

The destination bank account could have been checked against the vendor master record in seconds.

The process simply never required anyone to perform that final independent verification.

AI recommendations should accelerate human review—

never replace it.

---

# TERMINOLOGY RECAP

### Automation Bias

The tendency for humans to accept AI recommendations without performing independent verification.

### Data Completeness Indicator

Every AI recommendation should communicate not only what was checked but also what was **not** checked.

High confidence does not imply complete coverage.

### Evidence-Based Review

Critical decisions should always be validated against the original source documents rather than relying solely on generated summaries.

### Independent Verification Gate

A mandatory review step for high-impact fields such as bank accounts, payment amounts, or counterparty identities before approval is enabled.

### Human-in-the-Loop

A human reviewer remains responsible for the final decision and independently verifies critical evidence before approving execution.
