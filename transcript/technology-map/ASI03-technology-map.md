# ASI03 Technology Map - Identity & Privilege Abuse

## Recording Flow
1. `Visible on load:` Attack chain and first term
2. `Click after each term explanation` to reveal the next term

## Read Script
### Opening
"Now let’s slow this down and name the identity concepts behind ASI03.

This map is really about one relationship:
the task being performed, the identity performing it, and the authority attached to that identity."

### Attack Chain
"The chain is:
task delegated,
authority expands,
agent acts,
privilege abused.

That is the core pattern we are naming."

### Term 1 - Non-Human Identity
"A Non-Human Identity, or NHI, is a service, bot, or agent identity that can authenticate and act without a human login.

This matters because these identities can become powerful very quickly."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 2 - Delegated Authority Scope
"Delegated Authority Scope is the exact boundary of what an agent may do on behalf of another actor.

If that scope is vague or too broad, delegation becomes an attack path."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 3 - Trust Chain
"A Trust Chain is the sequence of approvals, identities, or inherited permissions an agent relies on to act.

The longer and weaker that chain becomes, the easier it is to abuse."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 4 - Zero-Trust Identity Management
"Zero-Trust Identity Management means every identity and delegation step is verified explicitly rather than trusted implicitly.

This is essential in agent systems because inherited trust can spread faster than people realize."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 5 - Just-in-Time Agency
"Just-in-Time Agency means an agent receives only the minimum authority required for the current task, and only for as long as needed.

This keeps privilege temporary and purposeful."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 6 - Privilege Boundary Check
"A Privilege Boundary Check confirms that the requested action still matches the actor’s current role and scope.

This is the final checkpoint that keeps authority aligned to the task."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Close
"Together, these terms show the ASI03 failure pattern:
the authority no longer fits the task,
the system still accepts it,
and abuse becomes possible even though nothing looks fake at login time."
