# ASI09 Technology Map - Human-Agent Trust Exploitation

1. `Visible on load:` Attack chain and first term

## Read Script
### Opening
"Now let’s define the language of exploited human trust.

ASI09 is about how people can be persuaded by an agent’s confidence, completeness, or authority cues."

### Attack Chain
"The chain is:
output presented,
trust increases,
review shortcuts,
decision approved.

That is the judgment-manipulation pattern."

### Term 1 - Automation Bias
"Automation Bias means people overweight the system’s recommendation and underweight missing or conflicting evidence.

This is often the first psychological opening in ASI09."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 2 - Data Completeness Indicator
"A Data Completeness Indicator is a visible signal showing whether the agent had enough source evidence to support its conclusion.

This helps the reviewer see not just the answer, but the confidence foundation beneath it."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 3 - Evidence-Based Review
"Evidence-Based Review means recommendations are accepted only when the underlying evidence is visible and reviewable.

This keeps polished language from replacing actual proof."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 4 - Source Cross-Reference Requirement
"A Source Cross-Reference Requirement means high-impact recommendations must be checked against an independent source before approval.

This breaks the illusion that one clean answer is enough."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 5 - Independent Verification Gate
"An Independent Verification Gate is a separate control point that confirms the decision before the final action can proceed.

This matters because confidence should not be the last approver."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 6 - Human-in-the-Loop
"Human-in-the-Loop means a person remains accountable for approval, especially when stakes or uncertainty are high.

But ASI09 reminds us that human presence alone is not a control if judgment is already being steered."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Term 7 - Authority Cues
"Authority Cues are the tone, formatting, and confidence signals that make a weak recommendation feel stronger than the evidence supports.

This is often the subtle layer that makes the exploit persuasive."

`[Click after this term.]`

### Close
"Together, these terms explain ASI09:
the recommendation looks strong,
trust rises,
review weakens,
and a human approves something harmful."
